## Frequently Asked Questions

### Device Requirements
**Q: What devices are compatible?**  
A: Motion Tracker requires a device with LiDAR sensor:
- iPhone 12 Pro, 13 Pro, 14 Pro, 15 Pro, 16 Pro, 17 Pro (and Pro Max models)
- iPad Pro 11-inch (4th generation or newer)
- iPad Pro 12.9-inch (4th generation or newer)
- iPad Pro 13-inch

Standard iPhone and iPad models do not have LiDAR and cannot run this app.

### Getting Started

**Q: How do I track motion?**  
A: 
1. Grant camera permission when prompted
2. Choose a tracking mode (Manual Point or Color Tracking)
3. Select a target by tapping on screen
4. Press "Start Recording" and move your device or target
5. Press "Stop Recording" to view graphs

---

**Q: What's the difference between Manual Point and Color Tracking?**  
A:
- **Manual Point:** Tap to select a fixed point (wall, person, object). Best for large targets.
- **Color Tracking:** Tap a colored object to track it automatically. Best for small, bright objects like tennis balls.

### Using the App

**Q: Why can't I see any graphs?**  
A: Make sure you:
- Selected a target before recording
- Moved the device or target during recording
- Stopped the recording to view graphs

**Q: How do I export my data?**  
A: After recording, tap "Export Data" to save as CSV or copy to Desmos.

**Q: What does "Copy to Desmos" do?**  
A: Formats your data for the Desmos graphing calculator and copies it to your clipboard.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: App says "LiDAR sensor required"**  
A: Your device does not have a LiDAR sensor. This app only works on iPhone Pro and iPad Pro models with LiDAR (see compatible devices above).

**Q: Camera permission denied**  
A: Go to Settings → Motion Tracker → Camera → Enable

**Q: AR tracking isn't working**  
A: 
- Ensure good lighting
- Avoid reflective or transparent surfaces
- Make sure camera lens is clean
- Try selecting a point with good contrast

**Q: Color tracking loses the object**  
A:
- Use bright, solid colors (yellow, orange, green work best)
- Ensure good lighting
- Keep background different from target color
- Avoid very small objects
